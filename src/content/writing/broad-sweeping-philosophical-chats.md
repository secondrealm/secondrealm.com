Social media has revolutionized the way we communicate, share ideas, and engage in debates. From X (fka Twitter) threads to Instagram stories, philosophical discussions have found a new home. But as much as I appreciate the democratization of discourse, I often find myself exasperated when these platforms are used for broad, abstract philosophical debates. Here's why:

### Lack of Practical Application

Scrolling through my feed, I often come across philosophical debates about the value of art or the morality of market behaviors. While intellectually stimulating, I can't help but wonder: How does this help anyone navigate the complexities of digital art market or the Web3 space?

### The Endless Loop

Without the constraints of face-to-face interaction, discussions can spiral without ever reaching a conclusion, leaving participants and observers alike feeling unsatisfied.

### Miscommunication

The brevity demanded by social media communications can lead to oversimplification. Philosophical nuances get lost in the sea of asynchronous conversation, leading to misunderstandings and debates that miss the mark.

### Over-Complexity

While some use social media for light-hearted content, others dive deep into philosophical intricacies, making it challenging for the average user to engage or even comprehend.

## When Broad Philosophical Chats on Social Media Make Sense

Despite my reservations, there are moments when these online debates shine:

Awareness Building:Social media can introduce complex topics to a broader audience, making philosophy more accessible to the masses.

Diverse Perspectives:The global nature of social media means participants from different backgrounds can offer unique insights, enriching the debate.

Quick Feedback:If you're testing a new idea or seeking diverse opinions, the immediacy of social media is unparalleled.

## Why I Prefer Practical Social Media Engagement

For me, the beauty of social media lies in its ability to offer practical solutions, share experiences, and create communities. I'm more inclined to engage with content that provides actionable tips for improving my online presence, stories that resonate with my experiences, or communities that support my interests.

While the vast digital landscape of social media offers a platform for all kinds of discussions, I find myself gravitating towards the practical and actionable. It's a reminder that in the age of information overload, finding content that truly resonates and offers value is the ultimate goal.
