The world of Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) has exploded in popularity, bringing with it a myriad of opinions, innovations, and misconceptions. One voice that stands out in the cacophony is that of Eric Rhodes, who has consistently shed light on the intricacies of NFT storage on the blockchain. Through a series of comments and discussions, Eric offers insights into the misconceptions surrounding NFT security and permanence.

#### The On-Chain Myth

One of the most persistent myths in the NFT space is the belief that all NFTs are stored "on-chain." Eric emphasizes that many are still coming to terms with the fact that their NFT images might not be stored on the blockchain. He notes, "I've been saving files locally since 2020. The myth that everything was 'on-chain' was cleverly perpetuated almost since day 1."

This misconception can lead to potential vulnerabilities for collectors who believe their digital assets are securely stored on the blockchain when, in reality, only the metadata might be.

#### Exceptions to the Rule

While the general rule is that the metadata is stored on-chain and the actual art might not be, there are exceptions. Eric points out projects like Avastars or Pixel Chain that have taken a different approach to storage. However, these are outliers in a sea of NFT projects that rely on third-party image hosting.

#### The Semantics of "On-Chain"

In the ever-evolving world of NFTs, terminology can often lead to confusion. Eric clarifies his use of the term "on-chain," stating that he uses it to mean minted on the chain. He emphasizes that "most digital assets aren't stored on-chain at this time." This distinction is crucial for collectors and artists to understand, as it impacts the security and longevity of their digital assets.

#### Solutions in the Horizon

Despite the challenges, there are solutions on the horizon. Eric mentions services like ClubNFT that automate the process of saving files locally, ensuring that collectors have a backup of their digital assets. Additionally, platforms like Arweave offer more permanent storage solutions, aiming to provide data storage in perpetuity.

#### The Broader NFT Landscape

Beyond the technicalities of storage, Eric touches upon the broader NFT art market. He believes that success for artists in this space often boils down to "hustle, audience, access, and awareness" rather than the specific blockchain used. He also emphasizes the importance of understanding the basics of blockchain over copyright when navigating the NFT art world.

In conclusion, as the NFT landscape continues to evolve, it's essential for both artists and collectors to be informed. Voices like Eric Rhodes play a crucial role in dispelling myths and guiding the community towards a more secure and transparent future in the world of digital art and collectibles.
