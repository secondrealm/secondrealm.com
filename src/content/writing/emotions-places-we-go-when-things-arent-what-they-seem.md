Drawing from my academic studies into emotional intelligence, emotional vocabulary, and emotional literacy, I'm connecting emotions with colors, flowers, and sentiment. The aim is to raise emotional awareness and express complex feelings visually. This creative journey, influenced by decades of personal growth and artistic practice, looks at how art and emotion overlap, fostering empathy and understanding. As I navigate this journey, I explore both collective and personal emotional expressions, contributing to a broader conversation on emotional intelligence and artistic expression. This pursuit goes beyond art creation; it's a journey into emotional intelligence that nurtures deeper connections and communication.

Palette Name: Illusion's Hue

Palette Sentiment: Mostly Neutral

Palette Description: The color palette for the "Places We Go When Things Aren't What They Seem" dives into the complexities of perception, the unexpected twists of reality, and the multifaceted nature of our experiences. Each color selected offers a unique lens through which to explore these themes. Together, these colors weave a narrative that acknowledges the complexity of navigating a world where appearances can be deceiving and realities are multifaceted. They remind us of the richness of human experience, the value of questioning our perceptions, and the importance of embracing the unexpected with curiosity and openness. This palette not only illustrates the emotional and cognitive landscapes we traverse in such moments but also celebrates the depth and diversity of understanding they can bring.
