As artists and creators, we constantly seek new avenues for self-expression and innovation. Over the years, technology has played a significant role in shaping the art world, revolutionizing the way we create, share, and experience art.

However, when it comes to AI, skepticism often arises. Many artists question whether AI can truly enhance their creative process or if it diminishes their role as artists.

In this post, I’ll explore the world of AI and its potential as a transformative tool for artists. So, let’s set aside our doubts and explore the possibilities together.

## AI as a Tool, Not an Entity

Before we dive in, it's crucial to clarify our understanding of AI. In the context of art, AI should be perceived as a tool rather than an independent entity with agency.

When we talk about AI, we are not referring to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), but rather really, really smart programs. By embracing this perspective, we can alleviate some of the fears associated with AI—fears often shaped by its portrayal in pop culture (think *Terminator*).

## The Artist's Craft

Throughout history, technology has continuously influenced the art world. The advent of the internet, blogging platforms, Photoshop, digital cameras, ebooks, and social media has all transformed the creative process.

However, amidst these changes, the artist's craft remains at the center. Just as a tool does not define the artist, a basketball player is not defined by their shoes. It is the artist’s talent, skills, and effort that shape the outcome.

## The Role of the Artist

While AI-generated art introduces new possibilities, it’s important to recognize that the artist still sits behind the work.

The artist is the one who directs the tool—shaping its output and infusing it with intention.

We can draw parallels to tools like Photoshop or digital cameras. Using them doesn’t automatically make someone a photographer. It’s the intentional use of the tool to communicate something meaningful that defines the artist.

## Expanding Boundaries

AI should be approached with curiosity.

It’s another tool—one that can expand creative horizons and challenge traditional boundaries. Throughout my own creative journey, I’ve explored ways to integrate AI into my process, experimenting with GANs and generative artwork.

These tools don’t replace creativity—they extend it.

As artists, we shouldn’t shy away from new tools.

By recognizing AI as something shaped by human intention, we can use it to expand what’s possible—pushing the boundaries of our craft while staying rooted in what makes art human.

The opportunity isn’t to replace the artist.

It’s to give the artist more to work with.
