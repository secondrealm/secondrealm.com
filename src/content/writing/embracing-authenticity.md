Steven Pressfield’s *Turning Pro* was more than just a book; it was a transformative read. This book isn’t just about professional success—it’s a manifesto for living authentically. As an artist and lifelong learner, I found Pressfield’s insights incredibly relevant.

---

## The Battle Between Ego and Self

Pressfield’s discussion of the ego versus the Self resonated deeply with me. He suggests that our greatest internal struggle is between these two forces. The ego, often loud and demanding, seeks immediate gratification and recognition, while the Self is our true essence, seeking fulfillment and purpose.

In my life, this battle manifested in various ways. My pursuit of careers in marketing, design, and tech; completing my bachelor’s degree; my journey as an artist; and my exploration into different artistic mediums—all were influenced by this internal conflict. The ego pushed me toward external validation—money, the halo effect, certificates, degrees, and creative recognition. In contrast, my Self yearned for authentic expression and meaningful connection through art.

This led me to a crucial realization: the importance of aligning my actions with my true Self. It meant recognizing when the ego was steering my decisions and gently redirecting my focus toward what genuinely resonated with my inner values and passions.

---

## Overcoming Resistance as a Path to Authenticity

*Turning Pro* dives into the concept of resistance—a force that prevents us from doing our work and living our truest lives. For me, resistance showed up as fear, procrastination, and self-doubt, especially in my artistic endeavors.

Pressfield’s solution is simple: turn pro.

This doesn’t just mean having a profession. It means adopting a professional mindset—commitment, consistency, and dedication to one’s craft. It means showing up every day, regardless of the circumstances, and doing the work.

Applying this principle, I began to approach my art with greater seriousness and intention. Whether it was navigating different career paths, creating digital paintings, or exploring new mediums, I committed to showing up. This shift helped me move through resistance and create work that felt more authentic and resonant.

---

## Living a Life of Purpose and Fulfillment

Finally, *Turning Pro* underscores the importance of living a life of purpose. Pressfield suggests that turning pro isn’t just about success—it’s about discovering and committing to your true calling.

For me, that calling has always been art.

My journey across different careers and creative paths was, in hindsight, a search for purpose. Pressfield’s book reminded me that fulfillment comes from pursuing what genuinely matters.

This realization brought clarity to my goals. My pursuit of money, prestige, education, and recognition wasn’t just about achievement—it was about understanding the human condition, which ultimately deepened my work as an artist.

Exploring different mediums—digital art, watercolor, new technologies—wasn’t just experimentation. It was a way of finding new forms of expression for my inner world.

---

## Closing Reflection

*Turning Pro* acted as a mirror—reflecting both my struggles and my aspirations.

It challenged me to confront my ego, move through resistance, and commit to a life rooted in purpose and authenticity.

As I continue my journey as an artist and lifelong learner, these ideas remain foundational—guiding how I think, create, and move forward.
