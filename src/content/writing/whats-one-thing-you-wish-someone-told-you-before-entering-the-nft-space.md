# Crypto Art Question of the Day: Lessons from the NFT Space

*Originally discussed [via Twitter on February 22, 2021](https://x.com/secondrealm/status/1363908015674507271?s=20)*

---

The content in this article comes from the archive of my irregular Twitter video series, **Crypto Art Question of the Day (#CAQOTD)**. I asked a group of veteran NFT artists: *“What’s one thing you wish someone told you before entering the NFT space?”*

Here are the insights from those who were minting on-chain before the massive boom.

---

## ⛽ The Reality of Gas Fees

> "LOL how much gas was going to be! First nft I sent to someone gas was .39 cents!!"
> — [@dcost11](https://twitter.com/dcost11)

Lately, we’ve seen crazy gas fees. It wasn’t long ago when gas was considerably cheaper, and many of us miss those days. While platforms are working on Layer 2 solutions (like Polygon and zk-rollups) to make minting more cost-effective, the current Ethereum mainnet remains a costly hurdle for newcomers.

## 😴 The "No-Sleep" Grind

When you’re an NFT creator, you are more than an artist. You are the marketing department, the community manager, and the partnership coordinator. 

> "you ain’t gonna sleep no more."
> — [@luluixixix](https://twitter.com/luluixixix)

## 🧘 Emotional Resilience

The market is a rollercoaster. It’s easy to get wrapped up in a successful project or crushed by a slow week. 

> "Keep breathing. Not all days are the same. Don't get too excited, don't ever get dejected."
> — [@cybershakti](https://twitter.com/cybershakti)

Colburn Bell adds that there is no "right" way to do this yet:
> "we're all just making it up as we go and there are no 'right' answers its about co-creating the future we want to see for artists and a return to ownership and empowerment."
> — [@colbornbell](https://twitter.com/colbornbell)

---

## ⚠️ The Shortcomings and Hard Truths

### Achievements are not Decentralized
A.L. Crego points out that your "Web2" or traditional art world success doesn't always carry over.
> "It doesn't matter what we have achieved years before Cryptoart... It's like I have to begin from 0."
> — [@alcrego_](https://twitter.com/ALCrego_)

### Recreating Old Systems
Many hope to disrupt the traditional art world, but some see the same old dynamics (scarcity, star systems, and "who you know") reappearing on-chain.
> "That this promising tech was going to be used to exacerbate the traditional art market dynamics we all meant to disrupt."
> — [@powerdada](https://twitter.com/PowerDada)

Sparrow echoes this sentiment:
> "Mostly it's actual promise has yet to be realised & what we currently see is just a recreation of the existing structures of the old art world."
> — [@blackboxdotart](https://twitter.com/blackboxdotart)

---

## 🔓 Empowerment and Independence

Despite the flaws, the power shift is real. You don't need a gallery's permission to be successful. 

> "You don’t have to get accepted onto any platforms or seek anyone else’s permission to be successful in the space. Doing it yourself on your own contracts is just as valid as any other platform."
> — [@jay_delay](https://twitter.com/Jay_Delay)

### Be Your Own Everything
In this space, an artist is their own gallery, curator, and brand ambassador. It is a massive amount of work, but the rewards—both financial and creative—are entirely yours. 

> "You're going to continually see people do 'better' than you and that's ok. Because your value isn't tied to anyone else but you. Make ART, Make connections, and HODL like your life depends on it."
> — [@reviiser](https://twitter.com/Reviiser)

---

## Final Words
Being a CryptoArt creator has its drawbacks, but the global market and the lack of traditional gatekeeping make the possibilities endless. Whether you are minting on a curated site or a permissionless one, it comes down to the hustle.

As Moxarra Gonzalez puts it:
> "Work your ass off it will be fun and profitable at some point. Enjoy the ride!!!!!"
> — [@moxarra](https://twitter.com/Moxarra)
